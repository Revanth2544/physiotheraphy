Cervical Collar

Detailed Explanation on Cervical Collars

Why to Use a Cervical Collar

A cervical collar, also known as a neck brace, is used to support and immobilize the neck. Here are the primary reasons for its use:

Injury Support: Following trauma, such as a car accident or a fall, a cervical collar helps stabilize the neck to prevent further injury.

Post-Surgical Care: After neck or spinal surgery, a cervical collar can help ensure proper healing by restricting movement.

Pain Relief: Conditions like cervical spondylosis, herniated discs, or severe muscle strain can benefit from the immobilization provided by a cervical collar.

Alignment Correction: For conditions causing misalignment of the cervical spine, a cervical collar can help maintain proper posture and alignment during the healing process.

When to Use a Cervical Collar

1.Acute Neck Injuries: Immediately following trauma to the neck, especially if there is a suspicion of spinal injury.

2.Post-Surgery: After surgical procedures on the cervical spine to protect the surgical site and support the healing process.

3.Chronic Conditions: In cases of chronic neck pain due to degenerative conditions or severe arthritis where movement exacerbates the pain.

4.During Recovery: To support the neck during recovery from severe muscle strains or ligament injuries.

How to Use a Cervical Collar

1.Proper Fit:
Measure the Neck: Measure the height and circumference of the neck to select the appropriate size.
Adjust the Collar: Follow the manufacturer's instructions for adjustments.

2.Wearing the Collar:
Front Closure: Position the front part under the chin and align the back part behind the neck.
Secure Straps: Use the Velcro straps or other fastening mechanisms to secure the collar in place.

3.Daily Use:
Duration: Follow your healthcare provider's instructions regarding how long to wear the collar each day.
Skin Care: Regularly check the skin under the collar for signs of irritation or pressure sores.
Hygiene: Remove the collar for cleaning if allowed, and maintain hygiene around the neck area.

4.Sleeping: Use a specialized pillow or maintain a straight neck position to avoid discomfort while sleeping with the collar.

Exercises to Avoid the Need for a Cervical Collar 1.Neck Stretches:

Chin Tucks: Gently pull the chin towards the chest and hold for a few seconds. Repeat several times.
Side Bends: Slowly tilt your head towards each shoulder, holding for a few seconds. Repeat on both sides.
Rotation: Turn your head slowly from side to side, aiming to look over each shoulder.

2.Strengthening Exercises:
Isometric Exercises: Push your head gently against your hand without moving your neck. Do this in all directions (front, back, and sides).
Resistance Band Exercises: Use a resistance band to provide gentle resistance while performing neck movements.

3.Posture Improvement:
Shoulder Blade Squeeze: Sit or stand with your back straight, squeeze your shoulder blades together, and hold for a few seconds.
Wall Angels: Stand with your back against a wall, and move your arms up and down in a "snow angel" motion, keeping your back flat against the wall.

4.Regular Physical Activity: Engage in activities like swimming, yoga, or Pilates that promote overall neck strength and flexibility.
